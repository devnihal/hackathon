Wood Carving

Master Craftsman of Kerala's Wood Carving Tradition

Gopalan Asari

Gopalan Asari is a distinguished wood carver from Kerala, renowned for his intricate designs that adorn temples and traditional homes. With a career spanning over four decades, Asari has significantly contributed to preserving and promoting Kerala's rich wood-carving heritage.​
Traditional Techniques and Styles :
Asari employs age-old techniques passed down through generations, specializing in deep relief carving and panel carving. His work often features motifs from Hindu mythology, floral patterns, and geometric designs, reflecting the cultural narratives of Kerala. Utilizing locally sourced hardwoods like teak and rosewood, his creations are celebrated for their durability and intricate detailing.
​ Contributions to Temples and Traditional Architecture :
Throughout his career, Asari has been instrumental in crafting wooden elements for temples, including pillars, panels, and ceilings. His work enhances the aesthetic and spiritual ambiance of these sacred spaces, showcasing the integral role of wood carving in Kerala's temple architecture. Additionally, Asari's designs feature in traditional Kerala homes, contributing to the state's architectural identity.
​ Preserving Cultural Heritage :
Beyond his craftsmanship, Asari is committed to mentoring young artisans, ensuring the continuation of Kerala's wood-carving traditions. His dedication not only preserves this art form but also supports the local economy by providing employment opportunities. ​ In summary, Gopalan Asari's work exemplifies the timeless beauty and cultural significance of Kerala's wood-carving traditions, reflecting the state's rich artistic heritage.
